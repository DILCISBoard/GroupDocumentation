1. Welcome - Secretary of the meeting (Janet)
2. Common questions
    1. Major revision planning. The topic for today is to consider the major revision, to be ready by the DLM spring meeting in 2027, and we need to plan this. We have thought about the AIP, we have done some updates to the AIP since 2019. The DILCIS Board is now just working voluntarily, no funding. Github is quiet which indicates that people are satisfied with the specifications. KB has been quietly working away at minor revisions (e.g. improving the text for clarity). The 3D CITS will go out for comment in parallel with the major revision. We will have to send out surveys for comments, but beware of survey fatigue. We need to remind people that we have been around for 10 years. New specifications need to be worked on by other experts - we cannot do this on our own. The new version of EAD (EAS suite) is available so we have something that will take a number of years, and we will need to keep supporting the previous versions. We discussed the goal and practicalities of becoming a CEN standard - we carry on with the major revision whilst the CEN process continues. We continue with the goal of having a CSIP with extensions instead of our current situation. The SIARD standard is not part of the major revision. We need to get the 3D CITS out for review with the EU survey. We then need to consider the CSIP, and follow up on AIUs etc. We have a lot of work. SM commented that EN standards have their own formats and styles, but are otherwise no problem for us (so for example STEP has many parts). MF stated that you have to read the CSIP if you are going to use the SIP, so putting them in one book would make sense. SM: put an intro into them all - the EN documents are in 5 parts, each charged for. Maybe we have shorter meetings (45 minutes) monthly to brainstorm to work out how to divide the specifications. The SIARD group needs time to carry out the major revision to 3.0. VV has not yet convened a meeting as there have been staffing issues, but have a new colleague starting in May to look after the SIARD Suite. The SFA is committed to SIARD and will be organising this in the coming year. The composition of the group is important - people with XML skills etc. MF: the highest priority is the right team - perhaps some stakeholders from Switzerland, Estonia, KEEPS etc. - get around 10 people. We are here to help with SIARD, and maybe a survey is needed? MF: there could be 3 teams - a core team, a team of writers, and a team of implementers, and make sure their names are on the website, plus the name of users. Who should work on CITS SIARD? A different group, with input from the end users, so a 4th group.  \
KB to SS - what about the AIC? SS - this is in connection with OCFL so the AIC can restore original file names and timings, and deal with deduplication, so there is better reference to things. We will have OCFL as part of the AIP. Or there could be better reference to items in the CSIP. SS has encouraged OCFL people to submit comments on Github. JA/DA suggestion of an EU survey (5 minutes, 3 questions) to look for silent E-ARK users.
